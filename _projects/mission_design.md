For ISAM technologies to be compatible with the constraints of modern spacecraft, a holistic design approach is needed. The SPARC lab focuses on the co-design of the construction process and the spacecraft, and develops frameworks for efficient ISAM missions. For instance, our work has shown:
 - In-space construction processes should use composites, polymers, and metals as feedstock materials to optimize thermal stability, resistance to disturbance loads, and minimal-mass buckling strength.
 - Among candidate manufacturing processes, deformation processes have the lowest specific energy consumption (<2 MJ/kg) and are thus well-suited for construction in space. {% cite bhundiya2022engineering %}
 - Design tradeoffs govern the fabrication time of large structures, e.g., five spacecraft can construct a 200-m diameter gridshell in 5 days with extrusion of composite tubes.
